## Why the Zen of Python matters

The Zen of Python is a compact set of principles you can see with `python -m this`. Beyond software, these ideas help teams coordinate and decide.

## Applying it to operations

### Make things explicit
- State tasks, owners, due dates, and expected outcomes in tickets and docs.
- Share facts with links. Capture decisions for later review.

### Prefer simplicity
- Start with the smallest working process and iterate.
- Keep roles to who/what/when first; expand only when needed.

### Readability counts
- Keep docs short, structured, and current. Add change notes.
- Summarize spoken agreements into written records.

### One obvious way
- Define a single default flow. Escalate exceptions explicitly.
- Examples: one form for inquiries, one payment path, one publish review.

### Now is better than never
- Ship drafts early and invite feedback. Normalize WIP.
- Use review and staged rollout for high‑risk changes.

## Implementation examples

- PR template with purpose, background, changes, impact, checks, reviewer, and due.
- Venue checklist on one sheet with owners and links to photos/layout.
- External comms: decisions logged; Slack posts carry a summary and links.
- Living FAQ: unanswered items become tickets to track.

## Common pitfalls and fixes

- Over‑process: trim rules that are not used; review weekly.
- Growing tacit knowledge: keep a one‑page onboarding always up to date.
- Fear of WIP: draft by default and make progress visible.

## Five‑minute starters

- Write your current task in one sentence on a ticket.
- List three decisions you want in the next meeting.
- Turn a fuzzy procedure into a one‑page checklist.
